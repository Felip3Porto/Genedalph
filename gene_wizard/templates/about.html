Genedalph is a web-tool dedicated to annotating long non-coding RNAs (lncRNAs) with functions

Traditionally, lncRNAs have been viewed as genetic “junk”, but we are now seeing their influence increase in neurological diseases, cancer, and heart disease. Yet, our understanding of lncRNA functional annotations is limited, and the reporting of these functional annotations are not easily accessible to experimentalists and health professionals.

Thereby, it is of the utmost importance to observe which genetic elements are dysregulated in the absence of a lncRNA, which can give further insights into understanding the functional composition and an overall break down of role of lncRNAs in cellular mechanisms.

Genedalph accomplishes functional annotation by hosting specially curated RNA-seq data from lncRNA knock down experiments, querying the most up to date pathway databases with the Gprofiler API, and providing a visualization of the different functional terms. Currently we host 43 different lncRNAs across 14 different human cell lines, which affects a total 32,674 unique genes.

We designed the database to be extremely simple to query differentially expressed genes across different cell lines, and made it simple to add future records of lncRNAs. If you don’t see a lncRNA you would like to see annotated: please contact us by sending a link of the paper of interest with RNA-seq data available.
